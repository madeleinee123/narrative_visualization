A look at the effects on mental health by isolation during the COVID-19 pandemic in the USA

After the start of the pandemic, many Americans started to feel the psychological effects of isolation. They were staying at home all the time and getting limited socially interaction and virtual no physical interaction. The National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) released a household pulse survey in late August 2020 to monitor the psychological effects of the stay at home orders on households across America. They sent out and are still sending out (as of August 2021) this survey everyweek and recording the percentage of people showing symptoms of anxiety, depression, or both.

Let's take a look at the results of this survey in comparison to recent years.

As you can see, there is a large increase in the percentage of the population that is experiencing these symptoms of depression and anxiety.
